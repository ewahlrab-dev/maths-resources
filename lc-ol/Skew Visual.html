Skewness in Histograms The skew is named after the tail — not the hump. Negative Skew LEFT-SKEWED ← tail ← TAIL POINTS LEFT Mean < Median RELATIONSHIP Most values bunch near the top, but a few low outliers drag the tail to the left. e.g. Age at death in a developed country No Skew SYMMETRIC / NORMAL balanced ← EQUAL BOTH SIDES → Mean = Median RELATIONSHIP Data is balanced around the centre. Tails are equal on both sides. e.g. Heights of students in a class Positive Skew RIGHT-SKEWED tail → TAIL POINTS RIGHT → Mean > Median RELATIONSHIP Most values bunch near the low end, but a few large outliers drag the tail to the right. e.g. Income — most earn modest salaries, a few earn millions 💡 The Golden Rule The name of the skew tells you which direction the tail points — not where the hump is. Positive = tail right. Negative = tail left. The mean always gets pulled in the direction of the tail, so it ends up on the same side as the skew name.
