The Empirical Rule

For a normal distribution, almost all data falls within three standard deviations of the mean.

μ ± 1σ
68%
of all data values
16% in each tail
Cut-offs: 16% & 84%
μ ± 2σ
95%
of all data values
2.5% in each tail
Cut-offs: 2.5% & 97.5%
μ ± 3σ
99.7%
of all data values
0.15% in each tail
Cut-offs: 0.15% & 99.85%
Key points: The mean (μ) sits at the exact centre — it equals the median and mode in a normal distribution. Each step left or right is one standard deviation (σ). The empirical rule only applies to normally distributed (bell-shaped) data. On the Leaving Cert you will most often use the 95% rule (μ ± 2σ).
