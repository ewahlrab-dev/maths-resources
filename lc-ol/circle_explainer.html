01

Equation of a Circle

Every circle has one equation. It comes directly from Pythagoras.

💡 The big idea: A circle is just all the points that are the same distance (the radius) from a fixed centre. That distance rule, written in algebra, is the equation of the circle.
Centre at the origin (0, 0)
x² + y² = r²
r = radius. Every point (x, y) on the circle is exactly r away from (0, 0).
Centre at point (h, k)
(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
Shift the centre away from the origin. h moves it left/right, k moves it up/down.

Why does Pythagoras give us this?

Take any point (x, y) on a circle centred at (h, k). Draw a right-angled triangle from the centre to that point. The horizontal leg is (x − h), the vertical leg is (y − k), and the hypotenuse is the radius r. Pythagoras says: a² + b² = c², so (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r². That's the whole thing.

02

Finding Centre & Radius

Given an equation, read off the centre and calculate the radius.

💡 The trick: Match what you're given to the standard form (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r². The centre is (h, k) and the radius is √(right-hand side). Watch the signs!
03

Inside, Outside, or On?

Test whether a point lies inside, outside, or exactly on a circle.

💡 The test: Substitute the point into the left-hand side of the circle equation. Compare the result to r². Less than r² = inside. Equal to r² = on the circle. Greater than r² = outside.
The rule
Substitute (x, y) → compare to r²
Result < r² → Inside
Result = r² → On the circle
Result > r² → Outside

Why does this work?

The left-hand side (x−h)² + (y−k)² is just the squared distance from the point to the centre. If that distance² is less than r², the point is closer than the radius — so it's inside. Simple as that.

Tangent to a Circle

A tangent touches the circle at exactly one point and is always perpendicular to the radius at that point.

💡 The key property: The radius to the point of tangency and the tangent line are always perpendicular (90°). This means their slopes multiply to give −1. Use this to find the tangent's slope.
45°

How to find the tangent line — step by step

  • Find the slope of the radius from centre to the given point: m₁ = (y−k)/(x−h)
  • The tangent is perpendicular, so its slope is: m₂ = −1/m₁
  • Use point-slope form with the tangent point: y − y₁ = m₂(x − x₁)
05

Line & Circle Intersection

Substitute the line into the circle equation and solve the resulting quadratic.

💡 The method: Get y (or x) from the line equation, substitute into the circle equation. You get a quadratic. The discriminant (b²−4ac) tells you: 2 solutions = 2 intersections, 1 solution = tangent, 0 real solutions = no intersection.

−1

The substitution method

If the line is y = mx + c and the circle is x² + y² = r², substitute y into the circle: x² + (mx+c)² = r². Expand and collect terms to get a quadratic in x. Solve by factorising or the quadratic formula. Each x value gives a point of intersection.
