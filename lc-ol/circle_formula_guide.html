The Equation of a Circle

A step-by-step guide to filling in the formula

The Formula — from your Maths Tables
(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
h = x-coordinate of centre
k = y-coordinate of centre
r = radius

What the letters mean on the circle

📋 How to Fill It In — 5 Easy Steps
1

Write out the formula first

Always start by writing the formula from your tables before you put any numbers in.

(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
2

Find h — the x-coordinate of the centre

Look at the centre point you are given. The first number is h.

Centre = (3, 5)  →  h = 3

3

Find k — the y-coordinate of the centre

The second number in the centre point is k.

Centre = (3, 5)  →  k = 5

4

Find r — the radius

The radius is the distance from the centre to the edge. You are usually given this directly.

Radius = 4  →  r = 4

5

Substitute in — swap the letters for numbers

Replace h, k, and r in the formula with the numbers you found.

⚠️ Don't forget to square the radius on the right-hand side!

✏️ Worked Examples
Example 1
Centre (3, 5), radius = 4
Step 2: h = 3 → replace h with 3
Step 3: k = 5 → replace k with 5
Step 4: r = 4 → r² = 4² = 16
Answer (x − 3)² + (y − 5)² = 16
Example 2 — watch the signs!
Centre (−2, 6), radius = 5
Step 2: h = −2 → x − (−2) becomes x + 2
Step 3: k = 6 → replace k with 6
Step 4: r = 5 → r² = 5² = 25
Answer (x + 2)² + (y − 6)² = 25
Example 3 — centre at the origin
Centre (0, 0), radius = 7
Step 2: h = 0 → x − 0 = just x
Step 3: k = 0 → y − 0 = just y
Step 4: r = 7 → r² = 7² = 49
Answer x² + y² = 49

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

✗
Forgetting to square the radius. The right-hand side must be r², not r. If r = 4, write 16, not 4.
✗
Sign errors with negative centres. If h = −2, then (x − h) = (x − (−2)) = (x + 2). A minus and a minus makes a plus!
✗
Mixing up h and k. h always comes first (x-coordinate), k always comes second (y-coordinate).
✗
Changing the formula. Keep the minus signs in the formula — (x − h) and (y − k). Do NOT write (x + h).

🧠 Remember — just fill in the gaps!

You are given the centre (h, k) and the radius r.

(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²

Swap each letter for the number you were given. Square the radius. Done! ✓
